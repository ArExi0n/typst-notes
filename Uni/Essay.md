## Essay 1

Entrepreneurship, to me, isn’t about selling a service or making an offer — it’s about creating real value for people. As the saying goes, “Value creation always comes before value capture.” That belief shapes how I build.

Today, I find myself working on my app but  not for money, but to remove friction from people’s lives to make complex systems simpler and inaccessible knowledge usable.

It started when I was deep into blockchain reading the Bitcoin whitepaper, exploring Solana, and understanding decentralized systems. That same evening, at my nephew’s birthday party, I joined a table of older relatives. My father’s older brother asked what I’d been upto because I was looking tired. When I mentioned crypto, he said it was a scam just gambling. That moment stayed with me. I realized I understood these systems, but many people around me didn’t not because they weren’t capable, but because of misinformation and fear.

That’s when I grabbed that opportunity. I began building an app using ML , AI models, trading bots , and trading automation to translate crypto complexity into simple, actionable insights for everyday users.

What makes me the one to do this is execution. I don’t just imagine ideas I ship them. I code, test, fail, and iterate fast. My technical skills, combined with persistence and curiosity, give me leverage to turn confusion into clarity.

At MIT and Stanford, I want to sharpen this mindset and learn to manifest and it would allow me to deepen my understanding of computer science, cryptography, and intelligent systems while learning from peers and mentors who challenge conventional thinking. Through Project Access’ 1-to-1 mentorship, I hope to refine my academic direction and strengthen my ability to articulate and pursue this mission effectively.
